# Signal Test — HYDRO THEORY
*A&R Evaluation Framework — Phantom Grid*

Run this test on every submission or candidate track.
Seven questions. Binary outputs. No partial credit.

---

## Instructions

Listen once without pausing. No notes.
Listen again, this time with the test open.
Answer each question with **YES** or **NO** only.
Do not qualify. Do not explain during the test.

---

## The Test

### Q1 — TRANSMISSION
> Does something travel in this music that could not have been transmitted any other way?

A track that could have been made by anyone transmits nothing specific. A track that could only have been made by this person, in this moment, from this place — transmits. This is the primary question. Everything else is secondary.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q2 — VOICE
> Is there at least one moment in this track that only this artist could have made?

Fluency is reproducible. Voice is not. You are not listening for technical skill or genre correctness. You are listening for the irreducible thing — the decision, the sound, the structure — that marks this as coming from a specific consciousness rather than a general competence.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q3 — LINEAGE
> Does this add to the lineage or does it consume it?

Music that references Detroit Electro without bringing anything new consumes the lineage. Music that has absorbed the tradition and moved — even slightly — beyond it, adds to it. This is not a question about originality for its own sake. It is a question about whether the music earns its references.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q4 — NECESSITY
> Would the Phantom Grid catalog be diminished without this release?

The question is not whether the music is good. The question is whether Phantom Grid is specifically the right home for it — and whether the catalog would be weaker without it. A yes here means the music fits the system's logic. A no means it may be excellent music for a different context.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q5 — FLOOR
> Does the production meet release standard without requiring label intervention?

Phantom Grid does not develop artists at the production level. The music arrives or it does not. Release standard means: the mix translates across systems, the master has headroom, the format is correct. This is not about perfection. It is about whether the work is done.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q6 — TIME
> Will this track hold its position in five years?

Trend-adjacent music fails this question immediately. Music that is genuinely rooted — in its own logic, in its own place, in its own moment without chasing the current moment — passes. This is a gut question. Trust the first answer.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

### Q7 — VOID
> Would Void play this?

Not at every show. Not in every context. But is there a context — a specific room, a specific moment — where Void would reach for this record? This question anchors the test to the label's human center. The label exists in cyberspace. The music plays in rooms.

`[ ] YES  [ ] NO`

---

## Scoring

**Non-negotiable — all three must be YES:**
- Q1 TRANSMISSION
- Q2 VOICE
- Q3 LINEAGE

If any of Q1, Q2, or Q3 is NO: **REJECT. Do not continue.**

**Threshold — minimum 3 of 4 must be YES:**
- Q4 NECESSITY
- Q5 FLOOR
- Q6 TIME
- Q7 VOID

**Result:**

| Score | Decision |
|---|---|
| Q1+Q2+Q3 = YES, Q4–Q7 ≥ 3 YES | **PASS — initiate A&R conversation** |
| Q1+Q2+Q3 = YES, Q4–Q7 < 3 YES | **HOLD — revisit in 90 days or on next release** |
| Any of Q1, Q2, Q3 = NO | **REJECT — close file** |

---

## After the Test

**PASS:** Hand off to Void with the completed test and one paragraph on why the three non-negotiables are met. No pitch. Facts only.

**HOLD:** Log in `transmission-log.md` with date and hold reason. Set a 90-day review.

**REJECT:** No response required beyond a standard note if contact was made. Do not explain the system to the artist.
